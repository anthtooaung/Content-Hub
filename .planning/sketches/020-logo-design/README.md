# Sketch 020: Logo Design

## Design Question
What logo mark best represents Content Hub as a playful, friendly hub connecting multiple social platforms?

## How to View
open .planning/sketches/020-logo-design/index.html

## Variants
- **A: Connected Nodes** — Central blue hub with platform-colored satellite dots (TikTok black, Instagram pink, Facebook blue) connected by curved lines. Emphasizes the "connection" metaphor directly.
- **B: Stacked Layers** — Three overlapping rounded rectangles in platform colors, fanning out like content cards. Emphasizes the "content" aspect — posts stacking and flowing through a hub.
- **C: Orbit Ring** — Central "CH" monogram with an elliptical orbit ring and platform-colored dots circling it. Emphasizes dynamic movement and a solar-system feel.
- **D: The Overlap** — Three semi-transparent platform-colored circles overlapping in a triangular arrangement. Where they intersect becomes the hub. The most organic, visually rich concept — color mixing creates new hues at intersections.
- **E: Lettermark** — Custom "CH" monogram where the C wraps into the H's left stroke. Platform-colored dots accent the letter terminals. Typography-based, classic, scales perfectly at any size.

## What to Look For
- **Scalability** — Does the mark read clearly at favicon (16px) vs header (32px+)?
- **Platform recognition** — Can you identify TikTok, Instagram, Facebook from the colors alone?
- **Playful vs professional** — Which concept balances friendly with credible?
- **Dark mode** — How does each mark work on dark backgrounds?
- **Icon-only use** — Which mark stands alone without the wordmark and still feels like "Content Hub"?
