# Sketch 018: Facebook Post Card

## Design Question
What does a pixel-perfect Facebook feed post card look like when displaying AI-generated content with/without user-uploaded images?

## How to View
open .planning/sketches/018-facebook-post-card/index.html

## Variants
- **A: With User Image** — Full Facebook post card with avatar, author name, timestamp, post text, uploaded campaign image, reactions bar (like/love/haha), comment section with sample comments. The "complete" experience.
- **B: No Image (Prompt)** ★ Selected — Same card structure but with an "Add an image to this post" placeholder where the image would go. Shows how the card handles the no-image case.
- **C: Feed Context** — The generated post shown between two other posts in a simulated Facebook feed, with a "Generated by Content Hub" badge. Shows how it fits in context.

## What to Look For
- Does the card feel like a real Facebook post or a styled approximation?
- Is the "Add image" placeholder in Variant B clear without being jarring?
- Does the "Generated" badge in Variant C feel trustworthy or suspicious?
- Are reactions, comments, and action buttons proportionally correct?
